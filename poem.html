transience

by Shona kelly

there’s a piano on my roof

a chime and composition with every drop

that falls fast

falls slow

i try to etch the notes on paper

on the lines, between

but the sound is stagnant by my hand

i don’t think the notes belong to me, my keys

i won’t ask why so i just listen

yearn

for the piano on my roof

the notes slide down my window

bounce on the ground

seep through the cracks on my roof

to the floor

i touch the pools

try to feel the notes

the timbre the stave

but the composition is gone

only a sound when falling

hitting a surface above

beyond

soundless, when whole

i hear the notes lighten to piano from forte

tempo pared to pause

then stop

i swish my fingers over the surface

ripples of sound no more

and I yearn

there are days of silence that stretch to weeks

more

and i wonder about the piano on my roof

if I should find it

play it

make the transience of its notes myself

then that thought is gone

when I hear the swell of music once more

a prelude of petrichor

a taste that catches in the back of my throat

maybe I should sing

because there’s a piano on my roof

and I’m in love with the one who plays


Gloucestershire Writer's Network Competition Winners 2024
